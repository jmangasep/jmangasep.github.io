About Me

Hi, I'm Janna (pronounced like "john-uh"), and I am a data storyteller and researcher from a suburb outside Memphis, TN. I am a recent graduate of Tulane University in New Orleans, LA, and I currently reside in Chicago, IL as I pursue my Master in Public Policy at the University of Chicago.

I am expected to graduate from the University of Chicago Harris School of Public Policy in June 2023. After graduation, I aim to be in an analytical/research role, particularly for an organization that strives towards equity and encourages a collaborative working environment.

I am most interested in the following policy areas (though this list continues to grow): education, reproductive rights, racial and gender equity, and public transit. I value the DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) framework and believe all researchers ought to incorporate this mindset at every step of the process.

I have had the privilege of working for several phenomenal organizations during my time at Tulane and at the University of Chicago. Here is a glimpse of what I've done over the past five years:

Research Roles

Undergraduate Researcher - Tulane University (2021)

Thesis Director: Dr. Mirya Holman

I successfully wrote and defended my undergraduate honors thesis on the effects of conservative morality and abortion misinformation on pro-life, single-issue voting behaviors. Using multivariate regression analysis in R, I analyzed the results of my 50-question nationwide survey of over 1,800 participants to find an association between single-issue voting behaviors and holding abortion misperceptions (e.g. falsely believing that obtaining an abortion leads to a higher likelihood of infertility). On the "front-end" of this work, I explored the constrictions faced by female-bodied people in reproductive health services, particularly those from historically disenfranchised communities.

Undergraduate Research Assistant - Tulane University (2020)

Principal Investigators: Dr. Mirya Holman, Dr. Claire Daniel, and Dr. Anna Mahoney

I assembled a spreadsheet of over 600 crisis pregnancy centers (CPCs) with qualitative elements of each: misinformation on abortion and contraception, location, contact number/email, etc. In this work, I discovered several dominant patterns of CPCs' anti-abortion rhetoric, one of which was posing abortion as a dangerous medical procedure. Though the spread of health misinformation continues to be disconcerting, I value this experience as understanding anti-abortion tactics of the pro-life bloc begets a better understanding of how we may fight against these harmful centers.

Undergraduate Research Assistant - Tulane University (2018)

Principal Investigators: Dr. Mirya Holman and Dr. J. Celeste Lay

Under the tenured professors Dr. Mirya Holman and Dr. Jennifer Celeste Lay, I analyzed flyers mailed to New Orleans residents during 2017 mayoral election to evaluate the impact of interest groups in a mayoral race in which many variables were controlled (i.e. race and gender). I also coded various aspects of the flyers (e.g. tone, issues mentioned, etc.) through Microsoft Excel. Overall, I had the pleasure of discovering tactics utilized by campaigns, political parties, and interest groups in order to promote/attack certain candidates and encourage voting on certain initiatives and amendments.
